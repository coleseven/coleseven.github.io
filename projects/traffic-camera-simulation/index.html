Traffic Camera Simulation

Overview

This project explores polling strategies for large-scale traffic camera systems. The goal was to reduce duplicate image downloads, minimize bandwidth usage, and avoid missing images under real-world timing constraints.

Problem

Polling dozens of cameras at fixed intervals leads to inefficiencies such as duplicate images, wasted bandwidth, and synchronization issues.

Approach

I built simulation models using Monte Carlo techniques to compare different polling strategies, including staggered polling and conditional requests.

Results

The simulations demonstrated measurable reductions in duplicate downloads and improved bandwidth efficiency under realistic timing distributions.
